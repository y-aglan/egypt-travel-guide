Egyptian History

Egypt has a rich and complex history spanning thousands of years. Key historical periods include:

  • Pharaonic Period - The era of the great Pharaohs, from the Old Kingdom to the New Kingdom, known for monumental architecture such as the pyramids and temples.
  • Greco-Roman Period - Following Alexander the Great's conquest, Egypt became a center of Hellenistic culture under the Ptolemaic dynasty, culminating in the Roman annexation.
  • Islamic Period - Starting in the 7th century AD, marked by the introduction of Islam and the establishment of Cairo as a major center of learning and culture.
  • Modern Era - The 19th and 20th centuries saw Egypt's struggle for independence, leading to its current status as a republic.
